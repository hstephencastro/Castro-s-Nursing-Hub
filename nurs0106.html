Pharmacology I

Foundations of drug therapy — from pharmacokinetics to clinical application

Course Overview

What You'll Learn

Pharmacology I introduces the foundational principles of drug therapy for nursing practice. You'll develop the clinical judgment skills needed to safely administer medications, anticipate drug effects, and educate patients — using the CJMM framework throughout.

💡 Professor Castro's Study Tip
Don't try to memorize every drug name — focus on the drug CLASS. If you know how loop diuretics work, you know how furosemide, bumetanide, and torsemide all work. Same goes for antibiotics, beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors. Learn the class, the rest follows.
